White Tiger

In the wild, white tigers occur once in every 10,000 births. - ifaw.org

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris tigris
  • Conservation Status: Endangered by traits
  • Average Lifespan: 20 years
  • Symbolisim: Strength

The White Tiger is a variant of the Bengal or Siberian tiger, known for its striking white fur with black or dark brown stripes which results from a recessive gene that affects pigmentation. Unlike albino tigers, they have blue eyes, a pink nose, and creamy white fur. They are predominantly found in captivity, as their unusual coloration makes them more susceptible to challenges in the wild, such as camouflage difficulties.
